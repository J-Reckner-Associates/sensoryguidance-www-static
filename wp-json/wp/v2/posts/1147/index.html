One of the most common uses of sensory research is qualifying a new supplier. Particularly now, when your current supplier may be struggling to keep up with demand, supplier qualification leveraging sensory research may be critical. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Here’s a case study in which our client was qualifying a new supplier of mustard oil, a key ingredient in their product.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Background<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Our client was looking to qualify an alternate supplier of mustard oil. We needed to determine if a sensory difference exists between the current product and a prototype made with the alternate supplier\u2019s mustard oil.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Methodology<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Using two samples – the Current Product and the Prototype – we conducted a Discrimination Test (2-AFC with Reference). The products were placed with 36 panelists at our facility in Harrison, NY.  Sessions were held in the sensory booths, and data was analyzed at 90% confidence level for a two-sided 2-AFC test. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Results<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n
