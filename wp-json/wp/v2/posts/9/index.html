The purpose of category appraisals is to benchmark against competitors and identify sensory space and market gaps of a product category.\u00a0 Here, some best practices to put in place on your next one:<\/p>\n

The Do’s<\/h2>\n
    \n
  1. Discuss your business objectives and previous research findings with the
    \nresearch team. In addition to helping you understanding the scientific process
    \nand data analysis, the research team ultimately seeks to help you make strong
    \nrecommendations that answer business questions.<\/li>\n
  2. Diversify the sensory space by including products that represent a wide
    \nvariety of sensory characteristics. This will help you to achieve a holistic
    \nunderstanding of the category and find product gaps and new product
    \nopportunities in the market place.<\/li>\n
  3. Remember that Drivers of Liking are calculated within the space defined by
    \nthe products included in the test. Therefore, it is a good practice to include
    \nat least one competitive product that you think may perform better than your
    \nproduct if you are looking for opportunities to improve your product.<\/li>\n
  4. Use the same serving protocol for Consumers and Trained Panelists.
    \nPanelists may additionally test products in non-consumer ways (e.g., eating
    \nsyrup without a waffle).<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    The Do Not’s<\/h2>\n
      \n
    1. Push the Trained Panel to profile on sensory characteristics that your team
      \nbelieves are important. The Panel Leader can address your concerns during
      \ntraining, but it is best to not bias the Panel.<\/li>\n
    2. Schedule consumer testing and sensory profiling more than a couple of
      \ndays apart. The age of the samples should be the same for Consumers and
      \nTrained Panelists.<\/li>\n
    3. Include too many or too few products. The ideal number of products to
      \ninclude in a category appraisal is eight to fifteen.<\/li>\n
    4. Include samples that are extremely divergent or that profile on the opposite
      \nspectrum from samples selected for category appraisal. The data extreme
      \ncharacteristics of the sample profile do not allow for good differentiation of
      \nother samples and can skew the data to one side.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
